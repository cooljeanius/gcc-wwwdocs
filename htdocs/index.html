Welcome to the egcs project!

egcs is an experimental step in the development of GCC, the GNU C compiler. If you are new to the egcs project, you should probably read the egcs mission statement.

We want to work closely with contributors to help and encourage them to submit their changes for inclusion in egcs. The egcs project provides access to its development sources with weekly snapshots and anonymous CVS. All code continues to be assigned to the FSF exactly as before and is available to the gcc2 maintainers for ultimate inclusion into the gcc2 tree.

We will provide regular, high quality releases. We want those releases to work well on a variety of native (including GNU/Linux) and cross targets and use an extensive test suite as well as a benchmark suite to maintain and improve quality. egcs-1.1.2 is the current release. gcc-2.95 is the next major release.

egcs is pronounced "eggs", the c is silent.

News/Announcements

May 27, 1999
Toon Moene has emailed (and posted) his notes on the GNU Fortran (g77) Birds-of-a-Feather (BOF) session at LinuxExpo to the appropriate lists, and Craig Burley has made Toon's notes available (in edited form) at http://world.std.com/~burley/bof.html.
Probably the most important decision reached at the meeting is that Craig Burley will undertake the long-awaited 0.6 rewrite of the g77 front end as his top priority for the gcc 3.0 release, rather than focusing on implementing some of the most wanted features that didn't require the rewrite, such as Cray pointers.
The BOF provided us with some additional information to guide future development of GNU Fortran. Thanks to all who attended, whether in person or in spirit!
May 18, 1999
The sixth snapshot of the ongoing re-written C++ Standard Library has been released. It includes SGI STL 3.2, an automatically generated <limits><\tt>, a partially re-written valarray, a working stringbuf and stringstream (for basic types). For more informtaion, please check libstdc++-2.90.4.
April 23, 1999
g77 now supports optional run-time checking of array subscript expressions via the -fbounds-check compiler option. (The same option applies to whatever bounds-checking applies for other languages, such as Java. The -ffortran-bounds-check option specifies bounds-checking for Fortran code.)
April 20, 1999
Yes, it is not a hoax: The egcs steering committee is appointed official GNU maintainer for GCC; the egcs team will be responsible for rolling out future GCC releases.
This will require some changes in policy and procedures for the project. We will provide more information on those changes as they are available.
www.gnu.org has the FSF announcement under the "GNU flashes" heading.
April 15, 1999
Mark Mitchell is now a co-maintainer of the C++ front end along with Jason Merrill.

Development snapshots and CVS Access

The egcs project makes weekly snapshots of its development sources and provides remote CVS access to its source tree.

The snapshots are available from ftp://egcs.cygnus.com/pub/egcs/snapshots/ and many mirror sites around the world.

Readonly CVS access is available for everyone on the internet, and read-write CVS access is available for major contributors.

Be aware that snapshots and CVS provides direct access to development sources, which can be unstable at times. If you are using the development sources, we recommend you sign up for one or more of the egcs mailing lists.
