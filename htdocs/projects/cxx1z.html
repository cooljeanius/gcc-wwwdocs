C++1z Support in GCC

GCC has experimental support for the next revision of the C++ standard, which is expected to be published in 2017.

C++1z features are available as part of "mainline" GCC in the trunk of GCC's repository and in GCC 5 and later. To enable C++1z support, add the command-line parameter -std=c++1z to your g++ command line. Or, to enable GNU extensions in addition to C++1z extensions, add -std=gnu++1z.

Important: Because the final ISO C++1z standard is still evolving, GCC's support is experimental. No attempt will be made to maintain backward compatibility with implementations of C++1z features that do not reflect the final standard.
